About Me

Hello, my name is Cameron Fox. I am a Computer Science student at the University of New Mexico, born and raised in Albuquerque, New Mexico. My passion for computer science has been with me for as long as I can remember. However, I didn't start coding until after graduating high school. Before my coding journey began, I was always intrigued by electronics—whether it was photography, video games, or learning how computers were built.

The first time I wrote code, I felt like I had truly found my calling. My first program was in Java—a simple "Hello, World!" script—but it sparked an excitement in me that has yet to fade. Since then, I have loved discovering new and creative ways to code. Over the years, I've realized that I enjoy building projects with a visual component. There's something incredible about creating an application that anyone—regardless of their coding knowledge—can interact with and see as a polished, professional product.

Outside of coding, I love spending time with my family and friends, whether it's a cozy night in or an exciting adventure. I have a deep appreciation for the outdoors and enjoy backpacking, hiking, and snowboarding in the mountains of New Mexico. I'm also a huge fan of sports—whether it's playing basketball, watching soccer, or attending live games, I love the energy and excitement that comes with it. When I'm looking for a more laid-back experience, I enjoy photography, exploring new restaurants, or getting lost in a great movie or video game.

Ultimately, I strive to balance my love for technology with my passion for adventure, creativity, and human connection. One of the key ways I do this is by incorporating creativity and a sense of human connection into everything I code.
