I'm Cameron Fox, a Computer Science student at the University of New Mexico with a passion for building software that blends creativity, usability, and clean engineering. Born and raised in Albuquerque, I’ve always been drawn to technology, whether it was photography, video games, or taking apart electronics just to see how they worked.

After high school, I wrote my first line of code in Java, a simple “Hello, World!”, and instantly knew I had found something special. I love creating projects with a visual, interactive component, especially ones that feel polished and intuitive to anyone who uses them. I enjoy making ideas come alive through software.

Outside of coding, I’m happiest spending time with the people I care about, whether it’s a cozy night in, exploring new restaurants, or heading out on an adventure. I love the outdoors: backpacking, hiking, snowboarding, and enjoying the beauty of New Mexico’s mountains. I’m also a huge sports fan who loves basketball, soccer, and attending live games whenever I can. And when I want something more laid-back, you’ll find me enjoying photography, movies, puzzles, or video games.

My goal is to build software that feels thoughtful, human, and creative. Tools that help people and bring ideas to life. I approach every project with curiosity, determination, and the belief that there is always more to learn.
